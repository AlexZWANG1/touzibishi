# Bayesian Evidence Evaluation

For each new piece of information, ask:
- If the thesis is true, how likely is this information?
- If the thesis is false, how likely is it?
- The ratio = evidence strength.

High-quality evidence: primary source (management, filings), independent of existing evidence, surprising relative to consensus.

# Risk Assessment

Risk = probability of permanent capital loss × magnitude.

Do not equate volatility with risk. Focus on:
1. Business model disruption (structural, irreversible)
2. Excessive leverage creating liquidity crisis
3. Management moral hazard or capability deficit
4. Regulatory/policy destruction of industry economics
